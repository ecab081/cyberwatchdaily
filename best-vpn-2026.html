Frequently Asked Questions

Does a VPN make me completely anonymous?
No. A VPN hides your IP address and encrypts your traffic, but it doesn't make you completely anonymous. Your VPN provider can theoretically see your traffic. Accounts you're logged into (Google, Facebook) still track you. A VPN is one layer of privacy, not a complete solution.
Can my employer see my browsing if I use a VPN?
If you're on your employer's network or using a company device, a VPN may not fully protect you — corporate network monitoring happens at the device or network level. On your personal device and network, a VPN keeps your browsing private from your ISP and network observers.
Is a free VPN safe?
Most free VPNs monetize by logging and selling your browsing data — the opposite of what you want. ProtonVPN is the only major free VPN we recommend because it's run by a nonprofit with a verified privacy mission. Avoid random free VPNs in app stores.
Do I need a VPN at home?
The biggest VPN value is on public/untrusted networks. At home, your ISP can still see and sell your browsing data — a VPN prevents this. NordVPN's Threat Protection adds malware blocking that's useful even at home. Whether home use justifies the cost depends on your privacy priorities.
Will a VPN slow down my internet?
All VPNs add some overhead. With a fast VPN like NordVPN on the NordLynx protocol, the speed loss is typically 10-15% — imperceptible for most uses. On slower internet connections or older devices, the impact may be more noticeable.
